Jessie Liu · Nada Hameed · Sarthak Agrawal · Luca Herden

Project Overview

We are building an interactive smoke simulation system where smoke forms letters and shapes, reacts to user-controlled wind disturbances, and gradually recovers its original formation. Our baseline is a 2D GPU-accelerated smoke simulator using Jos Stam's Stable Fluids solver, with a stretch goal of extending the same system into 3D.

The simulator currently runs in real time at 90 fps on a 128×128 grid. Smoke is emitted from letter-shaped masks ("CS184"), mouse movement injects wind, and buoyancy causes smoke to rise naturally. Live GUI sliders control emit rate, wind strength, buoyancy, and decay.
